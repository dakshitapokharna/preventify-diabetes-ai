# ICMR Standard Treatment Workflow for T2DM 2024

**Source:** ICMR_STW_2024
**Citation:** ICMR Standard Treatment Workflow: Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. New Delhi: ICMR; 2024
**Population:** Adult T2DM patients in India — reflects Government of India drug
formulary and what is actually prescribed in Indian public health (PHC/Aardram) settings.
**Scope:** Step-by-step treatment escalation, first-line therapy (Metformin positioning),
drug selection at each HbA1c threshold, complication screening intervals,
lifestyle modification protocols, and referral criteria.

> **Clinical priority note:** This is the most operationally grounded India source — it
> reflects GoI-approved drugs that PHC facilities stock. Use alongside RSSDI 2022 as a
> co-primary source. When ICMR STW and ADA 2026 conflict, prefer ICMR STW for drug
> availability / formulary decisions; prefer ADA for detailed clinical evidence grades.

## INVESTIGATION
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="INVESTIGATION" topic_tags="general" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- HbA1c
- Creatinine
- K +
- Fasting lipid profile
- Urine routine examination and spot albumin: creatinine ratio #
- LFT/ ALT, AST
- ECG
- Others like Echo, USG abdomen as indicated
- # These may best be carried out after initial glycaemic control

## MONITORING
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="MONITORING" topic_tags="general" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- Blood glucose; FPG and 2 hours PPG once monthly more frequent as required including SMBG or CGM
- HbA1c every 6-12 months ( 3 monthly if uncontrolled)
- Annual monitoring : ECG, urine ACR (albumin creatinine ratio),dilated fundoscopy,foot examination

## METABOLIC TARGETS
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="METABOLIC TARGETS" topic_tags="glycemic_targets, HbA1c" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- HbA 1 c </= 7 .0% (except elderly and those with significant comorbid conditions) where higher target may be acceptable
- Pre-prandial capillary plasma glucose: 801 30 mg/dl
- Post-prandial capillary plasma glucose: < 1 80 mg/dl
- BP=140/90 (130/80 in CKD) LDL: < 100 mg/dl (< 70mg/dl in CAD)

## REFERRALS
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="REFERRALS" topic_tags="referral, escalation" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- Endocrinology: for uncontrolled hyperglycemia
- Ophthalmology: at initial evaluation and every year
- Nephrology: for deranged renal function
- Cardiology: for CAD/HF/arrhythmia

## IN AN ADULT WITH ILLNESS
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="IN AN ADULT WITH ILLNESS" topic_tags="general" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- In any adult/adolescent who presents with one of the following illness/complaints
- Osmotic symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, nocturia)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Unexplained depression or dementia
- Acute coronary syndrome
- Deep seated infections (liver abscess, lower lobe pneumonia, tuberculosis, pyelonephritis, abscesses, septic arthritis, osteomyelitis)
- Recurrent infections (tinea, oral thrush, onychomycosis, cystitis-urinary tract infection, sinusitis, STI, cellulitis, carbuncle)
- Non-healing ulcers (foot ulcers-infected/neuropathic)
- Exogenous/iatrogenic Cushing's syndrome

## IN PREGNANCY
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="IN PREGNANCY" topic_tags="pregnancy, GDM" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- H/O GDM/Pre-existing diabetes
- All pregnant women to be screened in 1 st  trimester with FPG
- FPG ≥ 126 and/or HbA1c ≥ 6.5% to be considered pre-existing diabetes
- FPG between 92-125 to be considered as GDM
- All those women with normal screening in 1st trimester to get a 75 g-oral glucose tolerance test done at 24-28 weeks
- All GDM women to be tested 6 weeks post-partum and once every 3 years
- PREDIABETES: should be tested yearly

## IN AN APPARENTLY NORMAL ADULT
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="IN AN APPARENTLY NORMAL ADULT" topic_tags="general" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- In obese or overweight (BMI ≥ 27.5 or ≥ 23 kg/m 2 ) with any of the following risk factors
- First degree relative with diabetes
- History of cardiovascular disease
- BP (≥ 140/90 mmHg)
- Dyslipidemia (TG > 250 mg/dL, HDL <40 mg/dl in male, <50 mg/dl in female
- Physical inactivity
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
- Insulin resistance (acanthosis nigricans)
- Adults > 30 years of age
- Previous history of GDM

## TREATMENT
<!-- rag_metadata source=ICMR_STW_2024 section="TREATMENT" topic_tags="general" population="T2DM India" year=2024 -->

- Dietary modification
- Avoidance of tobacco and restriction/ avoidance of alcohol
- Physical activity
- Pharmacotherapy:
- ›  HbA 1 c < 8.5%: Monotherapy- Metformin
- ›  HbA 1 c 8.51 0%: Dual therapy- Metformin + SU's/TZD/ DPPIVi/SGLT2i /AGI/GLP1 RA
- ›  HbA 1 c > 1 0%: Basal Insulin+ Metformin + another OAD / triple OAD combination
